Outside the Box Game Jam Ideas (brainstorm doc)

My first thought when seeing the theme of outside the box is to try and
make a game that is truly unique. There could also be a literal theme
where maybe your goal is to escape a box. There is also the idea of the
core mechanic of a game being opening boxes of random packages and
having the result of that impact a core gameplay loop. Box itself can
also hold several references. A box can be an object that is used as
storage, it can be a reference to the shape of a cube itself, or it can
be a reference to a zone. I kind of like the idea of trying to stay out
of a zone, but this idea to me would likely require multiplayer
implementation of which I am simply not there yet.

Maybe having a moving zone could help to make a single player game
that's about staying outside of a zone, but it also feels like that may
make it like some mini games from party games. As I continue thinking, I
have a lot of ideas that either don't particularly excite me or seem
beyond the scope of completion within a month. Box also reminds me of
the bloxor game from childhood where the primary objective was to move a
rectangular prism through a hole and the complication was moving through
physical space. I could try a similar approach with slight changes, but
I also feel as though I've seen this done before.

Another thing to consider is the particular brand of game jam is
supposed to be bad ideas, where maybe the idea isn't fully thought out
but the execution makes a game fun. I could try and make a box
making/open factory style game, which I don't particularly hate. I enjoy
a good factory sim, and this would make creating a core logic loop in a
low amount of time. Factory sim games are pretty common but I could also
add a gambling factor by creating a range of results from opening boxes
as well

Another option that would kind of require my own game engine would be to
create a game where the majority of the gameplay takes place outside of
the game window, which would likely mean exclusivity to windows as well
as messing with a person's drive/OS, so I don't see this as an ideal
solution. I've also thought about doing boxcar racing which could be
fun, or I could try another escape game like a puzzle platformer to
escape a box.

The more I think about it though, the more I enjoy the idea of making a
factory sim due to the easy scalability of the scope of the game, the
ease of logic in making the game, as well as the endorphin release of
opening a mystery box that everyone loves, as well as the potential to
poke fun at the ridiculousness of mystery boxes.
