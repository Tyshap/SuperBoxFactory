Super Box Factory Game Design Document

**Introduction:**

Title: Super Box Factory

Concept: The game will be a factory simulator where the goal is to make
a factory that produces boxes that you sell. There will also be a
mechanic where you may open boxes to receive a random reward that could
be worth more or less than the selling price of the boxes.

Genre: Simulation Strategy

Target Audience: Satisfactory/Factorio Players

Platform: Windows (Expandable)

**Gameplay**:

The core gameplay loop may change a bit as you progress, but the general
idea is that you will obtain the materials to make boxes, make them,
then sell them. In the future, you will be able to make different kinds
of random mystery boxes that you could either sell as is, or open to try
and get a reward that's worth more than what the box itself could sell
for. Players will start with a certain amount of money and there will be
a line where if you ever drop below a certain amount, you will go
bankrupt (lose). This amount may scale with the overall worth of the
factory, but the general idea is the more machinery that is in the
factory, the higher the cost to run the factory will be which means that
if you are trying to scale up the size of the factory, you will need to
upscale the revenue of the factory as well.

**Key Objects:**

The objects that are required to make the game playable are the
materials for the boxes and the boxes themselves, as well as some form
of assembly to ensure that you can buy, make, and sell boxes. Next up
would-be objects that allow for automation, automatically obtaining
materials, automatically making the boxes, automatically selling the
boxes, and a way to move those boxes. There will also need to be an area
to build this system in that could be expandable. This alone should be
enough to create a simple gameplay loop that allows for expansion.

For more complex gameplay, I would like to create an object that
supposedly "Fills" the boxes with mystery objects that adds a flat rate
to increase the worth of the boxes. Another object could be added to
open those boxes to receive the random reward instead of simply selling
the boxes. It will be important to do the math to ensure that the cost
of running the machines is lower than the earnings received from using
them, as well as the rent. The reason for including these costs is so
that people can't just buy more space without increasing the number of
boxes they are selling, giving a more realistic progression than other
factory games.

**Art Direction:**

The art will be simple models that don't particularly have shading or
anything so that I won't have to worry about making complex or detailed
models or textures. Keeping the art simple will be crucial to completing
this in time. Similarly, sounds will be limited to a simple background
track, sounds for machines, and sounds for interaction with boxes.

**Objectives:**

The simple objective for most people would be to generate as much money
as possible, but I would also like there to be an actual achievement
list for people to accomplish if there is time for implementation. It
could range from total boxes sold, to unlocking specific features, or
even to the amount of production cost at once. If I have the time, this
would be a great way to give people a more concrete objective instead of
simply trying to earn as much money as possible.
